What Lives Here

Meet Our Residents

Molefe Wild Trails is home to a wide variety of animal species including Africa's most iconic mammals, hundreds of bird species, and a rich variety of reptiles and insects. Every visit brings a new and exciting encounter.

Most Iconic

The Big Five

The Big Five are Africa's most famous and sought-after animals. Molefe Wild Trails is proud to be home to all five.

African Lion

Panthera leo

Africa's most iconic predator. Our park is home to two resident prides. Best spotted at dawn during our guided game drives through the open plains.

African Elephant

Loxodonta africana

The world's largest land animal. Our elephant population thrives along the riverine woodlands. A truly humbling sight to encounter in the wild.

Leopard

Panthera pardus

The most elusive of the Big Five. Patient game viewers are rewarded with sightings of these solitary, nocturnal hunters resting in trees.

African Buffalo

Syncerus caffer

One of Africa's most dangerous animals, yet spectacular to observe in large herds near our water sources. A true symbol of African strength.

White Rhinoceros

Ceratotherium simum

Our rhino conservation programme is one of our proudest achievements. A symbol of our deep commitment to protecting endangered species in Botswana.

Also Found At Molefe Wild Trails

More Incredible Animals

Southern Giraffe

Watch these graceful giants browse the treetops at dusk, silhouetted against our spectacular Botswana sunsets. A favourite with all visitors.

Plains Zebra

Thousands of zebra populate our plains. Each zebra's stripe pattern is completely unique — like a fingerprint of the wild.

Cheetah

The world's fastest land animal. Our open plains provide perfect habitat for cheetah families. A breathtaking sight at full speed.

Hippopotamus

Hippopotamus amphibius

Africa's third largest land animal. Found wallowing in our rivers and watering holes, hippos are fascinating to watch from a safe distance.

Nile Crocodile

Crocodylus niloticus

One of Africa's most ancient predators. Our rivers are home to large Nile crocodiles that can grow up to 5 metres in length.

African Wild Dog

Lycaon pictus

One of Africa's most endangered carnivores with a hunting success rate of over 80%. A rare and exciting sighting at Molefe Wild Trails.

Spotted Hyena

Crocuta crocuta

Africa's most successful large predator. Highly intelligent social animals whose night calls echo across our plains after dark.

Warthog

Phacochoerus africanus

A firm favourite with visitors. Warthogs trot across the plains with their tails held high and are always entertaining to watch.

Greater Kudu

Tragelaphus strepsiceros

One of Africa's most elegant antelopes. Male kudus boast magnificent spiral horns that can grow up to 1.8 metres in length.

Impala

Aepyceros melampus

The most abundant antelope in Africa. Impalas can leap up to 3 metres high and 10 metres in length to escape predators.

Vervet Monkey

Chlorocebus pygerythrus

Playful and curious, vervet monkeys are a delight to watch in our riverine forests. Their alarm calls alert other animals to nearby predators.

Blue Wildebeest

Connochaetes taurinus

Large herds of wildebeest roam our plains year-round. Their dramatic stampedes are one of nature's most thrilling spectacles to witness.

Waterbuck

Kobus ellipsiprymnus

Always found near water, waterbucks are large sturdy antelopes with a distinctive white ring on their rump. A common but beautiful sighting.
